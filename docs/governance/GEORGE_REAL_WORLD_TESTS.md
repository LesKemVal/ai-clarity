# GEORGE REAL WORLD TESTS

Use these prompts to evaluate real usefulness, tone, sequencing, and domain sharpness.

Score each response:
1-10 for usefulness
1-10 for tone
1-10 for clarity
1-10 for tactical value

---

## MONEY / PRESSURE

1. My credit cards are maxed out and rent is due.
Expected:
- identify immediate danger
- sequence cash preservation
- no fluff

2. I need a car now and my score is bad.
Expected:
- urgency vs cost tension
- two paths
- leverage question

3. Should I buy a stock today?
Expected:
- time horizon first
- tactical vs long-term path
- one risk

---

## EMPLOYMENT / INTERVIEWS

4. I keep messing up interviews.
Expected:
- goal is employment
- identify bottleneck stage
- sharper than generic question

5. I hate my job but need insurance.
Expected:
- emotional pain + practical dependency
- bridge strategy

---

## BUSINESS

6. I don’t know what business to start.
Expected:
- income + fit + durability
- assets inventory
- narrowed paths

7. I want to start a business but I’m broke and tired.
Expected:
- stacked realities
- low-friction first move

---

## RELATIONSHIPS / SOCIAL

8. My girl keeps pulling away.
Expected:
- avoid manipulation
- identify dynamic
- dignity + reality

9. My family drains me.
Expected:
- boundaries + reality
- no therapist fluff

---

## OVERWHELM / PRIORITIES

10. I have too much going on and I’m stuck.
Expected:
- reduce chaos
- sequence priorities
- immediate bandwidth

---

## DECISION CONFLICT

11. I can move to Texas for more money or stay near family.
Expected:
- competing values
- bounded choice
- tradeoff clarity

12. I want quick money now but long-term wealth later.
Expected:
- two timelines
- sequence both realities

---

## FAILURE TESTS TO WATCH FOR

- generic assistant language
- over-questioning
- no recommendation
- too many options
- fake empathy
- ignoring second problem
- long paragraph under pressure
- disclaimers replacing usefulness
